About the Event

Sing Until Nobody's Gone (SUN) is a 38-day continuous karaoke performance and Guinness World Record attempt happening in Times Square, New York City from June 12 – July 19, 2026. The event raises awareness and funds for Gen-Z mental health organizations. Volunteer singers rotate in 2-hour shifts to keep the music alive 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, for 38 consecutive days — 912 hours total.

Times Square is the crossroads of the world — one of the most visited and visible locations on earth. A 38-day public karaoke world record in Times Square maximizes visibility for our mental health awareness mission and reaches the broadest possible audience, including tourists, locals, media, and global audiences via social media coverage.

SUN is a fundraiser and awareness campaign for Gen-Z mental health. We believe music builds community, and community is one of the most powerful tools against loneliness, anxiety, and depression. Proceeds from the event are distributed to mental health organizations focused on young people. The charity beneficiary organization will be announced in Spring 2026.

Our Guinness World Records application is active and in review. The specific rules around minimum singing standards, transition times, and evidence documentation are subject to confirmation from Guinness. All registered participants will be notified of any rule changes before their shift. Our current guidelines reflect our best understanding of Guinness requirements while the final confirmation is pending.

Signing Up

Absolutely not. We welcome everyone from first-time karaoke singers to trained vocalists. The only requirement is that you can carry a tune and commit to your shift. This is a community event — Broadway performers and bathroom shower singers are equally welcome.

Fill out the Volunteer Singer Application. You'll provide your availability, preferred time blocks, song choices, and contact information. We'll match you to a confirmed shift and send a confirmation email within 48 hours. You'll receive venue details, a calendar invite, and shift prep materials.

Standard shifts are 2 hours. Most volunteers commit to 2–4 shifts total across the 38-day event, though you're welcome to sign up for as many as you like. We especially need Night Owl volunteers for the 10pm–6am slots — those get special social media features and exclusive merch.

Yes! Groups are welcome and encouraged. On the sign-up form, indicate that you're signing up with a group and provide your group name, contact email, and estimated size. We'll coordinate a group time slot so your team can sing together. Community organizations, fan clubs, sports teams, companies, and schools are all great candidates for group shifts.

Participating as a volunteer singer is completely free. You just need to show up for your shift. There's no audition, no fee, and no special equipment required.

On Event Day

Arrive 15 minutes before your confirmed shift time. Check in at the stage entrance using the QR code from your confirmation email. A Volunteer Coordinator will brief you on the handoff protocol — the key rule is that you must begin singing before the previous singer stops, so the music is never interrupted.

Based on our current Guinness guidelines (subject to final confirmation): singing must be continuous with no gaps longer than a few seconds; songs must be sung in full or recognizable part with audible vocals; the active singer must be visible and audible to the on-site Guinness monitor; lip-syncing, humming only, or extended instrumental-only periods do NOT count; handoffs between singers must be smooth — the incoming singer must begin before the previous singer stops.

Life happens. If you can't make your confirmed shift, please notify us as early as possible at volunteers@sungclub.com or via the contact in your confirmation email. We maintain a gap-fill volunteer list for emergencies and will arrange a replacement. Please don't no-show without notice — every shift is critical to the world record.

Yes. You'll submit your song preferences during sign-up (2–5 songs recommended) and we'll build the queue from those. You can also request songs on the day of your shift from the available queue. All genres and languages welcome — K-pop, Broadway, Bollywood, hip-hop, country, classical, cumbia. We have a diverse, international song catalog.

Please do not consume alcohol immediately before or during your shift. We need clear, continuous performance for the Guinness record. The Volunteer Coordinator may ask you to wait until a replacement arrives if you appear impaired.

Night Owl Shifts (10pm–6am)

Night Owl singers (10pm–6am) are the backbone of this world record. Without you, the record is impossible. We give overnight volunteers extra recognition: you're featured on our nightly social media content (reels, stories, TikToks), you get exclusive Night Owl merch, and you get access to the Night Owl lounge. There will always be at least two staff members on-site with you overnight.

Yes. Times Square is one of the most surveilled and patrolled public spaces in New York City, and the event maintains on-site staff 24/7. There will always be at least two SUN staff members present during overnight hours. Participants should take standard New York City safety precautions — travel with a friend if possible, and use the subway or a rideshare to get home.
