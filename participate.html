How to Participate

Everything you need to know about joining the world record attempt. Sign up, show up, make history.

Shift Structure

When Do You Sing?

The event runs 24/7 for 38 days. Shifts are organized into three time blocks — pick what works for you.

7:00 AM – 2:00 PM

🌅 Morning

Start the day strong. Outdoor stage at Times Square. Perfect for early risers and those with evening commitments.

  • 7 hours of shift coverage
  • Outdoor stage (Times Square)
  • Best for: commuters, professionals, early birds
  • Quieter crowd — more intimate performance
2:00 PM – 10:00 PM

⭐ Prime Time

The highest energy block. Peak tourist traffic and the biggest crowds. This is the main event.

  • 8 hours of shift coverage
  • Outdoor stage (Times Square)
  • Best for: performers, extroverts, show-stoppers
  • Largest live audience of the day
10:00 PM – 7:00 AM

🦉 Night Owl

The shift that keeps the record alive. Indoor ballroom at the Edison Hotel. Critical — and comes with exclusive perks.

  • 9 hours of shift coverage
  • Indoor (Edison Hotel ballroom)
  • Best for: night workers, students, night owls
  • Exclusive Night Owl Club benefits
Your 2-Hour Slot

What happens during your shift

🚶

Arrive 15 minutes early

Check in at the volunteer desk with your QR code (sent after registration). Meet your shift coordinator.

📋

Get briefed

Your coordinator reviews the song queue, GWR rules, and introduces you to the other singers in your slot.

🎤

Sing your songs

You'll rotate through the queue with fellow singers. Each song: minimum 2 minutes (GWR rule), recommended 3–5 minutes.

☕

Breaks (GWR rules)

5 minutes per hour, or 20 minutes per 4 hours — as long as someone else is singing. The mic must never go silent.

✅

Check out & celebrate

Scan out at the desk at the end of your slot. You're now officially part of the record.

GWR Rules

The rules that make it official

Song Length
Minimum 2 minutes per song

Guinness requires full performances. Snippets don't count. Recommended: 3–5 minute songs.

Continuity Rule
Someone must be singing at ALL times

No gaps. When one singer finishes, the next must start immediately. This is what makes it a world record.

Song Selection
Any song, any language, any genre

K-pop, Broadway, Bollywood, country, hip-hop, classical — all welcome. The diversity is part of the story.

Documentation
Guinness adjudicator on-site

A licensed GWR adjudicator will be present throughout the event. Your shift is logged via SunLog (our check-in system).

📍 Venues
Outdoor Stage: Times Square, 42nd–47th St area (exact location TBC with NYC permits)
Indoor Venue: Edison Hotel Ballroom, 228 W 47th St, Manhattan (Night Owl / bad weather)

The only requirement is that songs are at least 2 minutes long (per Guinness World Records rules). You'll submit your song choices during registration so we can have the backing tracks ready.

Absolutely not. This is karaoke, not American Idol. We welcome singers of every skill level — first-timers, shower singers, tone-deaf enthusiasts, and seasoned performers alike. The world record is about community and continuity, not perfection. If you can hold a microphone and make noise for 2 hours, you qualify.

Participants must be at least 16 years old. Singers aged 16–17 require a signed parental consent form (available during registration and at check-in). There is no upper age limit — we actively welcome older singers, especially for daytime Morning shifts. The night shifts (10pm–7am) are 18+ only.

Zero cost. Singing is completely free. This is a volunteer event — you're the reason the record happens. We provide the stage, the sound system, the backing tracks, and the crowd. You just bring your voice.

Your registration QR code (we'll email it to you), a valid photo ID for check-in, comfortable clothes (you'll be standing and performing), and water. For outdoor shifts, dress for the weather — it's June/July in NYC, so expect heat. The Night Owl indoor venue is air-conditioned, so bring a light layer.

Life happens. If you need to cancel, please email volunteers@sungclub.com as early as possible so we can fill your slot. We maintain a backup singer roster specifically for last-minute cancellations — but the earlier you let us know, the easier it is for everyone. Repeated no-shows (without notice) may affect your ability to register for future shifts.
