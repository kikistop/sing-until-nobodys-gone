Event Summary

What is Sing Until Nobody's Gone?

Sing Until Nobody's Gone (SUN) is a Guinness World Record attempt for the longest continuous karaoke performance ever recorded. The event runs for 38 consecutive days — 912 hours — at a live stage in Times Square, New York City.

Volunteer singers from across New York and beyond rotate in 2-hour shifts, keeping the music alive 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. No singer performs alone — the microphone passes from voice to voice without interruption.

The event is a fundraiser and awareness campaign for Gen-Z mental health. The mission: use the power of music to demonstrate that community, connection, and creativity are lifelines — and that nobody has to struggle alone.

"Every voice matters. Every shift counts. Every song is a reminder that you are not alone."

About Sing Until Nobody's Gone

Sing Until Nobody's Gone (SUN) is a Guinness World Record attempt and mental health awareness campaign taking place in Times Square, New York City from June 12 to July 19, 2026. Over 38 consecutive days — 912 hours — volunteer singers from across New York City rotate in 2-hour shifts to maintain a non-stop karaoke performance, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

The event raises awareness and funds for Gen-Z mental health organizations. Open to all experience levels, all genres, and all backgrounds — the event celebrates the power of music, community, and human connection. For more information, visit sungclub.com or contact press@sungclub.com.
